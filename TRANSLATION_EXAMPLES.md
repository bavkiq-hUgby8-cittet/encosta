# Translation Examples - Portuguese to English Mapping

This document shows the creative translation approach used for the Touch? app i18n files.

## Philosophy

**Not literal translation** — Cultural and tonal adaptation for each language:
- English: Sophisticated, minimalist, mysterious but warm
- Spanish: Warm, elegant, natural phrasing
- Japanese: Respectful, subtle, concise
- Russian: Poetic, emotional depth

## Translation Principles Applied

1. **Preserve Intent Over Words**
   - "O acaso tem bom gosto" is not literally about taste
   - English "Chance has taste" captures the discrimination/selectivity idea

2. **Adapt for Language Idioms**
   - English prefers shorter, punchier sentences
   - Japanese uses polite registers (desu/masu)
   - Spanish maintains warmth through diminutives when appropriate

3. **Maintain Poetic Tone**
   - Keep metaphors where possible
   - Use parallel structure for power
   - Fragment sentences for emphasis (when appropriate for language)

4. **Cultural Resonance**
   - English emphasis on sophistication/minimalism
   - Portuguese original celebrates warmth and connection
   - Each language reflects its cultural aesthetic

5. **Consistency Within Category**
   - "First encounter" phrases: Optimistic, discovering tone
   - "Legendary" phrases: Elevated, historical tone
   - Zodiac combinations: Elemental language throughout

## Quality Checklist

For each translation:
- [ ] Captures original meaning/intent
- [ ] Appropriate tone for target language
- [ ] Readable and natural to native speakers
- [ ] Maintains poetic/metaphorical nature
- [ ] Consistent with app brand voice
- [ ] Special characters properly escaped in JSON
- [ ] Length reasonable for UI display (typically 5-15 words per phrase)
