The magic

Your speaker is the bridge

Touch? emits an ultrasonic signal through your phone's speaker - a frequency humans can't hear but phones detect instantly. When two phones are close enough, the speakers recognize each other and create a cryptographic handshake.

No internet needed for the detection. No Bluetooth pairing. No QR scanning between users. Just proximity and sound. The same technology that makes the physical world magical.

This is why we say "Touch?" - because the gesture of bringing phones close is the most natural, human way to say "I'm here, and I see you."

FAQ
Common questions
Do I need to download an app?
No. Touch? works in your browser. Go to touch-irl.com, create an account, and start connecting. No app store needed.
How does the sound connection work?
Your phone emits an ultrasonic signal through its speaker - a frequency too high for humans to hear. When another phone is close enough, it detects the signal through its microphone, creating a secure handshake. It takes about 2-3 seconds.
Is my data safe?
Yes. By default, people only see your nickname and a color. Your real name, photo, and social media are hidden until you explicitly choose to reveal them to specific people.
How do tips work?
After touching phones (or scanning a QR code), you'll see the option to tip. Choose an amount, pay with Apple Pay, Google Pay, or a saved card. The tip goes directly to the service provider's account.
What if the other person doesn't have Touch?
Show them your QR code. They scan it with their camera, sign up in 15 seconds (nickname, birthdate, email, password), and they're ready to connect. No app download needed.
Can I use Touch? for my business?
Yes. Service providers can receive tips, venue operators can manage check-ins and events, and any business can set up operator mode to run their daily routines through Touch?.
What are stars?
Stars are your physical reputation. You can earn up to 10 through achievements (100, 500, 1000 unique connections), purchases with score points, donations from others, or professional recognition. They're proof you build real relationships.
